Dosas (Indian-style Pancakes)

A dosa is a thin pancake or crepe, originating from the Indian subcontinent, made from a fermented batter. It is somewhat similar to a crepe in appearance. Its main ingredients are rice and black gram. It is somewhat similar to a crepe in appearance. Its main ingredients are rice and black gram.

  • Prep Time: 10 minutes
  • Cook Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 3

Ingridients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 cup idli rice (or parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Wash and soak urad dal, rice, and fenugreek seeds in 2 cups of water for 6 hours. Drain and grind into a smooth batter with 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Add water if necessary to get the desired consistency.
  2. Heat a nonstick griddle or skillet over medium heat, and grease lightly with oil. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle, and spread into a thin circle. Cook until the edges begin to brown, 2 to 3 minutes. Flip and cook until the other side is browned, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, and repeat with remaining batter.
